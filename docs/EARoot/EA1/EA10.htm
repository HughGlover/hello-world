<font color="#0070c0"><b><u>Definition</u></b></font><br/>A general representation of anything that is required or used for a process or activity.  Generally it will be something that is consumed in some sense.<br/><br/><font color="#0070c0"><b><u>Examples</u></b></font><br/>- Funding for a research study or for any other resource<br/>- Material such as:<br/>    #gt; a fridge<br/>    #gt; medical sundries, for example syringes<br/>    #gt; management tools such as a clinical trial management system<br/>- Labor, this could include trial monitors, nursing staff<br/>- Facilities or Services for example a pharmacy might be providing a resource of dispensing.<br/><br/><font color="#0070c0"><b><u>Notes</u></b></font><br/>A number of resources are modelled separately in CDM and should not be represented with the Resource class.  For example Investigators and other research staff would not be regarded as Labour Resources; similarly the Study Drug would not be Material resource.  Where a specific resource has attributes or relationships beyond those represented by the class it is reasonable to derive a specialised version of the class.<br/><br/>The Purpose of a resource provider or study site in a context can be inferred from the Resource type, for example purpose of a hospital department might be to act as a depot, or it may be a acting as a managemnet unit.<br/>
